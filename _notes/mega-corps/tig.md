# 1. Core Identity

**Name:** Tachibana Infrastructure Group  
**Common Slang / Nickname:** TIG, “Tig,” “The Grid”  
**Public Slogan:** *“Reliability, Refined.”*  
**Primary Industry:** Power Generation, Transmission, and Grid Management  
**Headquarters Location:** Sound City – Crown Spire Tower (Financial Crescent District)  

**Public Image:**  
> A vertically integrated utility ensuring long-term energy stability across Sound City and surrounding Coalition districts. TIG emphasizes modernization, resilience, and responsible consumption.

**Private Reality:**  
> TIG operates electricity as a subscription-based service ecosystem. Through incremental policy shifts, tier fragmentation, and dynamic pricing, they continuously extract more revenue while gradually reducing baseline service quality.

---

# 2. Origin Story (Contextual Background)

**Founded:** Early Reconstruction (via merger of regional utilities)  

**Historical Narrative:**  
TIG emerged from consolidation of several regional grid operators into a single vertically integrated entity. The Coalition granted a long-term charter in exchange for infrastructure modernization guarantees.

**Foundational Myth:**
- Unified fragmented post-collapse grid systems  
- Modernized transmission backbone  
- Invested heavily in smart grid technology  
- Reduced catastrophic blackout frequency  
- “Stabilized Cascadia’s future”
